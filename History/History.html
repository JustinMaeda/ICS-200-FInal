History


What is League of Legends?

Just another DOTA clone

League of Legends is a 5v5 Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) where teams compete to destroy the other team's nexus, which is a structure placed at the center of each team's base. It was released on October 27, 2009, in response to the massive popularity of a WarCraft III mod called Defense Of The Ancients (DOTA). DOTA's creation and subsequent success skyrocketed the popularity of the MOBA genre. Over the decade following many different high-budget MOBA games released to capitalize on its success, including League of Legends, Heroes of the Storm, and even DOTA 2; Valve's official MOBA created by the lead designer of the original mod.

Origin of LOL Esports

Humble Beginnings

League of Legends Esports began in mainly online tournaments and competitions. But as the game's popularity grew, tournaments for the game began getting hosted at in-person gaming events like WCG in 2010. Even the season 1 world championship was hosted at another event, as Riot Games (LoL's Creator) couldn't afford to officially host the event solo. But the game's esports scene would develop quickly as LoL tournaments would become huge draws for gaming event attendees.
