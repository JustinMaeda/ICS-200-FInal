LoL ESPORTS

International Tournaments


MSI

Mid Season Invitational

MSI, or Mid Season Invitational is one of the two yearly international tournaments for League of Legends. The tournament features 2 stages of 13 teams facing off in a double-elimination bracket for a combined prize pool of $250,000.

The teams are decided based on the results of each major regions 1st split. In League esports, the regular season is cut into two tournaments called splits, each taking around 5 months to complete. Following the first split of the year, the top 2 teams from each major region are invited to compete in MSI (8 teams). However, the No. 2 teams from each major region must compete against teams from minor regions in a smaller tournament stage known as Play-Ins for a chance to compete in the playoffs proper. Play-Ins consist of two groups of four teams playing best-of-3 matches in double-elimination format until the four teams that go either 0-2 or 1-2 are eliminated.

Playoffs are a very straightforward double-elimination stage featuring 15 best-of-5 matches.

WORLDS

League of Legends World Championship

The League of Legends World Championship Tournament or "WORLDS" is the largest international event for LoL. It consists of 3 stages; Play-Ins, Swiss, and Knockouts. Over 20 teams from around the world participate every year for a chance at the combined 2.23 million U.S. Dollars (as of 2023).

The Play-Ins stage of worlds is an opportunity for top teams from minor regions and lower ranking teams from major regions to compete in the main event. 8 teams comprised of 6 minor region representatives and 2 major representatives are split into two groups of double-elimination brackets. Once each group has an upper and lower bracket winner, those 4 teams play two final best-of-5s to determine which two teams advance to the main tournament.

In the Swiss stage, the remaining 16 teams will play up to 5 matches until each team has either 3 wins or 3 losses. The rounds that decide whether a team advances or is eliminated will be best-of-3, while the rest of the matches will be best-of-1s. Over the course of 5 rounds the swiss stage will be 33 matches

The knockouts stage serves as a typical playoff-style finale to the main tournament. The remaining 8 teams following the swiss stage will engage in a best-of-5 single elimination bracket of quarterfinals, semifinals, and finals to decide the winner of it all.
